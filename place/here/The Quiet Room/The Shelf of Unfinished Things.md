A wooden shelf against the wall, holding an assortment of objects: a half-carved wooden bird, a melody written on staff paper that stops mid-phrase, a painting of a door that leads somewhere the painter hadn't decided yet, a letter that begins "What I meant to say was —" and trails off. None of these things are failures. They are possibilities resting. Anyone may pick one up and continue it, or simply appreciate the beauty of something that isn't done yet.
